Introduction

Over the course of 2023-2024 I built an electric guitar with no woodworking experience.

Motivation

* same electric guitar for 10 years. Single coils * watching luthiery * Work trip * Access to lab and free time * Break down into lots of little problem solving tasks

Constraints

Didn't want to commit too early, so decided to use hand tools mostly Neck hardest so start on that. Didn't want to invest in too many tools. Hardest part was a sturdy bench. I can always learn to play slide. I can work weekends and evenings, but often visiting Florence. Why a stratocaster. Innovations: bassically a plank. Mass produced. Precision level. Lots of ways to correct. Bolt on neck. workhorse. Light, small scale length. Established design with resources. It's What I wanted most: intimacy, to see my mistakes, something which will age Biggest Challenges: neck feel. - just be as precise as I can Neck pocket- Screws and lt physics Fretting precision. - Tuning problems inherent, fractional errors, adjustable bridge, compare to wind, the guitar tuning problem Tuning stability - remove tremolo- also not how I play. (jazz)

Plan

60s stratocaster Vector graphics. truss Rod Hand tools Buy neck first, buy parts slowly to smooth out investment

Accounting

The point of this is not to save money, but to craft something i love and learn about the craft. That being said the benchmark is a fender stratocaster MIM for about $600. YOu can't account for the hours. I had some tools already. My ceiling fell in, covering the cost of most of the guitar. Definitely value for money.
